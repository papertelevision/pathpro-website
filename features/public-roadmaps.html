Build trust through transparency.

A public roadmap keeps your community informed, reduces “when is X coming?” support tickets, and shows users their feedback matters. When people can see what you’re working on, they trust you more.

  • Share what’s planned, in progress, and shipped — keep your community in the loop at every stage
  • Reduce repetitive support requests — point users to the roadmap instead of answering the same questions
  • Show users their ideas are being heard — promote community suggestions directly to your roadmap
  • Build confidence in your product direction — a visible roadmap signals an active, responsive team

Frequently asked questions

Is the public roadmap really free?

Yes. PathPro’s Free plan includes a fully functional public roadmap with status columns, community voting, and comments — no credit card required, no trial expiration.

Can community members vote and comment?

Absolutely. Anyone can view your public roadmap, upvote features they care about, and leave comments. Community members are always free and unlimited on every plan.

Can I use my own domain?

Yes, but only on paid plans. Each project gets a custom subdomain (yourproduct.pathpro.co) out of the box, and paid plans support full custom domains like roadmap.yoursite.com with simple DNS setup.

How do I notify users when a feature ships?

Include the feature in your release notes. When a release is published, users who voted or commented are notified and can review every included feature and update in one place.

Can I control what’s visible on the public roadmap?

Yes. You choose which items appear publicly. Internal tasks and sensitive work stay private while your community sees only what you want to share.

Does it integrate with my existing workflow?

PathPro roadmaps connect directly to your task boards, feature voting, and release notes — so your roadmap stays in sync with your team’s actual progress without extra work.
