Best Canny Alternatives for Feedback & Roadmaps (2026)

Feb 25, 2026 · 8 min read
JS
Josh Sears
Founder, PathPro

The best Canny alternatives in 2026 are PathPro, Productboard, UserVoice, and Nolt. Each platform lets you collect user feedback and share a public roadmap, but they differ significantly in pricing, built-in task management, and AI-powered features.

What does Canny do?

Canny is a feedback management tool that lets users submit feature requests, upvote ideas, and view a public changelog. It focuses exclusively on the feedback-to-roadmap pipeline. Teams use it to capture demand signals without building their own portal.

Why look for a Canny alternative?

Canny does feedback well, but many teams outgrow it for a few common reasons:

  • No built-in task management. You still need Jira, Linear, or Asana to track the actual work. That means syncing data between two systems.
  • Pricing scales quickly. Canny's Growth plan starts at $360/mo. Teams with tight budgets often look for more value per dollar.
  • Limited support tooling. If you also want a customer support portal alongside your feedback board, you need yet another tool.
  • No AI-powered analysis. Canny surfaces what users request, but does not analyze trends, detect duplicates, or score demand automatically.

Best Canny alternative for all-in-one product management

PathPro

PathPro replaces Canny, your task tracker, and your support tool in a single platform. You get feedback collection, a public roadmap, kanban task boards with subtasks, release notes, and a customer support portal. PathFox Product Intelligence analyzes feedback trends, detects duplicates, and scores demand automatically.

PathPro offers a free-forever plan with unlimited community members. Paid plans start lower than Canny's Growth tier and include everything — no add-ons required.

Best Canny alternative for enterprise feedback

Productboard

Productboard focuses on product strategy and prioritization. It excels at connecting customer insights to roadmap decisions. The platform offers a structured prioritization framework and integrates with tools like Salesforce and Zendesk.

Productboard is best for larger teams that need detailed prioritization workflows. It does not include task management or support tickets, so you still need separate tools for execution.

Best Canny alternative for large organizations

UserVoice

UserVoice targets enterprise teams with high-volume feedback needs. It offers SmartVote surveys, in-app feedback widgets, and Salesforce integration. Pricing starts around $799/mo, making it one of the most expensive options.

Choose UserVoice if your organization processes thousands of feedback items per month and needs enterprise-grade compliance features.

Best Canny alternative on a budget

Nolt

Nolt is a lightweight feedback board with a simple interface. Plans start at $25/mo for a single board. It supports custom domains and SSO, but lacks task management, roadmaps beyond a basic status board, and AI features.

Nolt works well for solo founders or very small teams who want a minimal feedback portal without extra complexity.

Which Canny alternative is best overall?

For most product teams, PathPro offers the best value. It covers the full product development lifecycle — feedback, roadmaps, tasks, releases, and support — in one platform. You eliminate tool sprawl, reduce costs, and get AI-powered insights that Canny does not provide.

If you need enterprise prioritization frameworks, look at Productboard. If budget is your only constraint, Nolt is the cheapest option. But for teams that want one tool instead of four, PathPro is the strongest Canny alternative available today.
